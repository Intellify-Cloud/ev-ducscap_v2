Buying a home starts with one important question: "How much home loan
can I actually qualify for?" It's one of the most searched questions by
South African home buyers, whether they're purchasing their first
property, upgrading to a larger home or investing in real estate. The
answer isn't based on your salary alone. Banks consider your
affordability, monthly expenses, existing debt, credit history,
employment stability, deposit and several other factors before deciding
how much they are prepared to lend. Understanding these factors before
you apply can save time, improve your chances of approval and help you
choose a property that fits comfortably within your budget. This guide
answers the most common questions South Africans ask about qualifying
for a home loan before explaining how affordability works in more
detail. \## Quick Answers to the Most Common Home Loan Questions The
questions below are based on what South African buyers ask most
frequently before applying for a home loan.

### How Much Home Loan Can I Qualify For?

There is no standard home loan amount that every buyer qualifies for.
Banks calculate affordability by assessing your income, monthly
expenses, existing debt, credit profile, employment history, deposit and
the property's value. Every lender has its own affordability model and
lending criteria. This means you may qualify for different loan amounts
from different banks. Working with a property finance specialist like
Duces Capital allows you to compare multiple lenders to determine which
bank is most likely to provide the most suitable home loan for your
financial circumstances. \### How Much Must I Earn to Qualify for a Home
Loan? There is no minimum salary that guarantees home loan approval.
Instead, banks assess whether your monthly income can comfortably cover
your existing financial commitments together with the expected bond
repayment. For example, two people earning R45,000 per month may qualify
for different loan amounts if one has vehicle finance, personal loans
and high credit card balances while the other has very little debt. Your
affordability matters more than your income alone. \### How Do Banks
Calculate Home Loan Affordability? Banks use several financial
indicators when assessing your application. These typically include:
Gross monthly income. Net monthly income. Existing monthly debt.
Household living expenses. Credit history. Employment stability.
Available deposit. Current interest rates. The expected monthly bond
repayment. The purpose is to determine whether you can comfortably repay
your home loan while meeting your other financial obligations. \### Does
My Credit Score Affect My Home Loan? Yes. Your credit score is one of
the most important factors lenders consider. A strong credit history
demonstrates responsible borrowing and may improve your chances of
approval while helping you secure a more competitive interest rate. A
weaker credit profile doesn't automatically mean your application will
be declined, but it may affect the amount you qualify for or the
interest rate offered. \### Can I Buy a House Without a Deposit? Yes.
Some South African banks offer 100% home loans to qualifying applicants.
However, providing a deposit can strengthen your application because it
reduces the lender's risk. A deposit may also: Improve your chances of
approval. Reduce the amount you need to borrow. Lower your monthly
repayments. Help you qualify for a more competitive interest rate.
Reduce the total interest paid over the life of your bond.

### Can I Qualify for a Home Loan if I'm Self Employed?

Yes. Self employed buyers qualify for home loans every day. However,
lenders usually require additional documentation to verify your income.
This may include: Business financial statements. Recent bank statements.
Tax returns. Management accounts. Proof of regular income. Preparing
these documents correctly before applying can significantly improve the
assessment process. \### Can a Bond Originator Improve My Chances of
Approval? A bond originator cannot guarantee home loan approval.
However, an experienced property finance company like Duces Capital can
improve the quality of your application by ensuring your documents are
complete, identifying potential issues before submission and presenting
your application to multiple participating lenders. Different banks have
different lending criteria. If one lender declines your application,
another may view your financial profile more favourably. \### How
Accurate Are Online Home Loan Calculators? Online affordability
calculators are useful for obtaining an estimate, but they cannot
replace a full affordability assessment. Most calculators only consider
a few financial variables. They generally do not account for: Your
complete credit profile. Individual bank lending policies. Existing
financial commitments. Current lending appetite. Supporting
documentation. Manual credit assessments. Treat online calculators as a
starting point rather than a guarantee of what you can borrow. \## Home

## Why Knowing Your Home Loan Affordability Matters

Many buyers begin house hunting before understanding what they can
realistically afford. While it's easy to focus on the purchase price of
a property, banks focus on something entirely different: your ability to
repay the loan consistently over the next 20 to 30 years. Knowing your
borrowing power before making an offer provides several advantages. It
helps you search within a realistic budget, strengthens your negotiating
position, reduces the risk of disappointing loan declines and allows you
to plan for all the costs associated with buying a property, including
transfer costs, bond registration fees and monthly ownership expenses.
Most importantly, it gives you confidence. Rather than wondering whether
a bank will approve your application, you'll have a clearer
understanding of what you can comfortably afford and which financing
options are likely to suit your financial circumstances. \## How Banks
Calculate How Much Home Loan You Can Afford One of the biggest
misconceptions among home buyers is that banks approve home loans based
mainly on salary. While your income is important, it is only one part of
the affordability assessment. When you apply for a home loan, banks want
to answer one fundamental question: "Can this borrower comfortably repay
the home loan every month while meeting all of their existing financial
commitments?" To answer that question, lenders examine your financial
profile from several angles. Understanding these factors before you
apply can help you improve your borrowing power and avoid unnecessary
disappointment. \### 1. Your Monthly Income Your income provides the
starting point for every home loan application. Banks consider all
regular and verifiable income, including: Basic salary Guaranteed
allowances Regular commissions Bonuses (where applicable) Rental income
Investment income Business income for self employed applicants Pension
income The higher and more stable your income, the greater your
potential borrowing capacity. However, income alone does not determine
how much you can borrow. \### 2. Your Monthly Expenses Banks also assess
how much of your income is already committed each month. These expenses
may include: Vehicle finance Personal loans Credit card repayments
Student loans Store accounts Child maintenance Insurance premiums
Monthly living expenses Other recurring financial commitments Your
disposable income is one of the most important figures in the
affordability calculation. Someone earning R70,000 per month with
significant debt may qualify for a smaller home loan than someone
earning R50,000 with very few financial commitments. \### 3. Your
Debt-to-Income Ratio One of the key measures banks use is your
Debt-to-Income (DTI) Ratio. This compares your monthly debt repayments
with your monthly income. A lower debt burden generally indicates that
you have greater capacity to take on additional borrowing. For example:
Monthly Income Monthly Debt Likely Outcome R40,000 R6,000 Stronger
affordability R40,000 R20,000 Reduced affordability

Although every lender has its own affordability model, reducing
unnecessary debt before applying for a home loan can significantly
strengthen your application. \### 4. Your Credit Record Your credit
report tells lenders how you have managed credit in the past. They look
at factors such as: Payment history Missed or late payments Existing
credit facilities Judgments or defaults Credit utilisation Overall
credit behaviour A positive credit history demonstrates responsible
financial management. This may improve both your chances of approval and
the interest rate offered. \### 5. Employment Stability Lenders prefer
borrowers with a stable source of income. Permanent employment generally
provides greater certainty than irregular income. That does not mean
contract workers, freelancers or self employed individuals cannot
qualify. It simply means banks may request additional documentation to
verify income consistency. For employed applicants this often includes:
Latest payslips Bank statements Employment confirmation For self
employed applicants it may include: Financial statements Tax assessments
Business bank statements Accountant prepared reports \### 6. The
Property You Want to Buy Banks also evaluate the property itself.
Factors include: Purchase price Market value Property type Location
Future resale potential Properties in established markets with strong
demand generally present lower lending risk than highly specialised
properties. The property therefore forms part of the lender's overall
risk assessment. \## How Much Salary Do You Need to Buy a Home? One of

Same Salary May Qualify for Different Home Loans Imagine two buyers who
each earn R55,000 per month. At first glance, you might expect both to
qualify for the same home loan. In reality, their outcomes could be very
different. Buyer A No vehicle finance. No personal loans. Small credit
card balance. Excellent credit history. Deposit available. Buyer B
Vehicle finance. Two personal loans. High credit card balances. Recent
late payments. No deposit. Although both buyers earn the same salary,
Buyer A is likely to qualify for a larger home loan and may receive a
more competitive interest rate. This example demonstrates why
affordability is about your complete financial picture rather than
income alone. \## Can You Increase the Amount You Qualify For? Yes.

the Whole Story Many prospective buyers begin by using an online home
loan or affordability calculator. These tools are useful for getting a
rough estimate of what you might be able to borrow. However, they have
important limitations. Most calculators rely on a few basic inputs, such
as income, loan term, deposit and interest rate. They cannot assess
factors that banks routinely consider during a formal application,
including: Your full credit profile. Existing debt obligations. Spending
patterns. Employment history. Self employed income structures. Current
lending policies. Individual bank risk appetite. As a result, two people
entering the same salary into an online calculator may receive the same
estimate, even though one ultimately qualifies for a substantially
larger home loan than the other. For this reason, calculators should be
viewed as planning tools rather than approval tools. A personalised
affordability assessment provides a far more accurate indication of what
you may qualify for and which lender is most likely to offer the most
suitable home loan. \## Frequently Asked Questions About Qualifying for

Although requirements vary slightly between lenders, applicants are
commonly asked to provide: South African identity document or passport.
Latest payslips. Recent bank statements. Proof of residence. Employment
confirmation. Offer to Purchase. Details of assets and liabilities. Self
employed applicants may also need to provide: Financial statements. Tax
assessments. Business bank statements. Accountant prepared financial
information. Preparing these documents in advance helps speed up the
application process. \## Why Home Loan Applications Are Sometimes
Declined A declined application does not necessarily mean you will never
qualify for a home loan. Banks decline applications for many different
reasons, including: Insufficient affordability. High levels of existing
debt. Poor credit history. Incomplete supporting documentation.
Irregular income. Property valuation concerns. Employment uncertainty.
Different lenders also have different lending policies. One bank may
decline an application that another lender is prepared to approve. This
is one of the reasons many buyers choose to compare multiple lenders
before accepting a final decision. \## Home Loan Application Checklist
